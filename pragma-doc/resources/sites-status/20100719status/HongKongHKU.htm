We try to list out some of our current work, please check if they are related to PRAGMA and can be used in your summary:
 
1. GeoScience:
Dr. Chun-Ho Liu, assistant professor of HKU Department of Mechanical Engineering and his research group are studying air pollutant transports and dispersion in urban street canyons using large-eddy simulation (LES). They have been working on the ventilation and pollutant removal mechanisms in various urban canopy layer conditions.
Their upcoming project objective is to study the air pollutant behaviors in urban areas and dense mega cities with an interdisciplinary approach which examine simultaneously the physics and chemistry processes. Multi-scale modeling will be used to investigate the interaction between the large meso-scale atmospheric processes and the small neighborhood-scale meteorological processes.

 

2. Resource & Data:

HKU is now focusing on building the regional Grid infrastructure: “HKU Grid Point system”. It is the largest (Grid) computing system in Hong Kong, with current theoretical processing power of 19.43TF and will be expanded to 27.05TF by early 2011. It is part of the China National Grid and is installed with Grid Operating System (GOS) as the Grid middleware. We are arranging an inauguration ceremony on 27th Aug 2010 at HKU to announce the opening of this Grid system (Detail please refer to http://www.hku.hk/cc/events/gridpoint/en/about/about.htm)

HKU is interested to join the PRAGMA VOMS and will plan to implement VOMS server at mid-2011.

HKU is also interested to implement PRAGMA Grid resource on virtualized platform at late 2011, e.g. replication of Rocks VM images to form a cluster. 

3. PRAGMA-20 preparation

HKU is now preparing the PRAGMA 20th workshop in March 2011, and the event title would be "PRAGMA 20 workshop and HKU Centennial celebration on Grid application and Research collaboration"

At the first day of the event it would be a GEO-science tutorial/workshop. We are inviting some local GEO-science speakers from Hong Kong Observatory, The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (HKPU) and The University of Hong Kong (HKU). They will present their detailed investigations in local (and Pearl River) region via different GEO-science aspects.

At the meanwhie, we are inviting sponsorship from some local IT companies to support this event and we are planning on some logistic preparation.
