All […] grids are encouraged to contribute their resources and technical support to MultiGrid experiments in GIN Testbed.

Non-member grids may be able to participate as well.[?] You may email to Phil Papadopoulos, […] for initial contact.

All participating grids are committed to a close collaboration within GGF Grid Interoperation Now (GIN) working group with their resources and technical expertise.

Here is the procedure for join the MultiGrid Interoperation Testbed:

1.     Identify one or more computer systems which can be modified for GIN, but still maintained as part of your own production grid, and satisfy the following minimum requirements:

a.     at least one front-end node

b.    at least two compute nodes

c.     at least one internet connection to the frond-end node

d.    at least 256MB RAM on each node

e.     at least 20GB disk space on each node

f.      running UNIX flavor operating system

g.     running Globus 2 or 3 or 4 (GT2 needs to be built with all SDK bundles - data, GRAM, Info - built with the same flavor), Globus libraries are available on both front-end and compute nodes

h.    running a job scheduler, such as SGE, PBS, SQMS or LSF

2.     Identify contact for your system(s), at least one management and one technical.

3.     Create an user account for MultiGrid Interoperation testbed monitoring

o       Download user account info pak

4.     Email the following info to Cindy Zheng

a.     your system(s) hostname / IP address

b.    if your clusters backend nodes are on a public network, the IP address range

c.     the info of the user account you just created for GIN testbed monitoring

d.    specifications of your cluster

e.     configuration of the globus toolkit

f.      GLOBUS_LOCATION

g.     available jobmanager

h.    your CA certificate and signing policy files

i.      URL to your system's users guide

j.      site contact (management) full name, email address, physical location (for time zone consideration)

k.    technical contact name, email address, physical location (for time zone consideration)

5.     Cindy will

o       add your site contacts to gin-ops mailing list

o       introduce you to gin-ops list

o       link your system user guide to GIN-ops web site.

o       Point you to the application requirements

o       Hook you up to the application driver(s)

From there on, you will be a part of the GIN testbed and working closely with GIN friends to add your site to the application run(s).
